Nails

A tiny macOS menu bar app that watches for nail biting and nudges you to stop.

Download v1.5.0 View Source
100% on-device processing

Requires macOS 15.0 (Sequoia) or later

Installation

1
Download & unzip

Download nails.app.zip from the releases page and move nails.app to your Applications folder.

2
Open the app

Double-click to open. macOS will show a warning because the app is not notarized.

3
Allow in System Settings

Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway to confirm your intent to open the app.

Features

👁

Real-Time Detection

Uses Apple Vision framework to analyze hand pose and face landmarks, distinguishing nail biting from other hand-near-mouth gestures.

🔔

Multiple Alert Types

Choose from sound alerts, a floating screen overlay, and system notifications. Each with its own configurable cooldown.

📸

Snapshot Capture

Optionally captures a photo on each detection so you can review them later and see your patterns.

🧠

Learns Over Time

Review detection snapshots and flag false alarms. The app adjusts its sensitivity based on your feedback.

🔒

Privacy First

All processing happens on-device. No data leaves your Mac. The camera is used non-exclusively so other apps work normally.

⚡

Lightweight

Runs at ~5 FPS with minimal CPU usage. Auto-pauses when your screen is locked. Lives entirely in the menu bar.

How It Works

Nails uses Apple's Vision framework to run hand pose estimation and face landmark detection on each video frame from your webcam.

It specifically looks for 1-3 fingertips near your mouth with fingers oriented toward it — the signature posture of nail biting. This avoids false alarms from resting your chin on your hand or covering your mouth.

When it catches you, it alerts you immediately so you can build awareness and break the habit.
